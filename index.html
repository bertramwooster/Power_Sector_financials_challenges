Econometric Methodology & Assumptions

1. The Demand Function (Logistic Decay)

Standard models assume compounding electricity demand. This simulation utilizes a logistic decay function to account for J&K's crashing Total Fertility Rate (TFR = 1.4) and impending demographic contraction. By the late 2030s, new household formation stalls, and affluent consumer defection to rooftop solar suppresses grid volume growth from a historical 6-8% CAGR down to near 0% by 2050.

2. The Cohort-Component Labor Model

The "Labor & Pensions" curve does not compound infinitely. It models a structural "Pension Peak" in the 2030s driven by the 8th/9th Pay Commissions and the mass retirement of the 1990s OPS (Old Pension Scheme) workforce. However, an actuarial mortality function deletes this liability mid-century, while new hires fall under the capped NPS. This creates the visible "Mortality Cliff" post-2040.

3. Sovereign Debt Compounding

The simulation isolates operational losses from capital obligations. The baseline legacy debt (from UDAY and Atmanirbhar Bharat) incurs a 7.5% servicing cost. Every year, the gap between Power Purchase + Labor and Revenue Realized is added to the principal UT debt. The resulting interest burden eventually eclipses the physical cost of running the utility, forming the "Debt Singularity."

4. Baseline Actuals (2000-2025)

Historical data anchors include verified AT&C improvements (from ~62% in 2005 to ~47% by 2025), staggered revenue realizations, and J&K UT general budget expansions mapped against state economic surveys and CAG audit baselines.
